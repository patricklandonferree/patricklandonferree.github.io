Profile

  • I study philosophy of the biomedical sciences at the University of Copenhagen. My PhD project explores epistemological and methodological dimensions of single-cell and spatial omics. I'm based at Medical Museion (a medical history museum) and affiliated with the Center for Medical Science and Technology Studies and the Novo Nordisk Foundation Center for Basic Metabolic Research. My advisors are Karin Tybjerg and Sara Green.

Biography

  • I grew up in Ramona, California. I attended Earl Warren College at the University of California in San Diego, where I double majored in chemistry and philosophy and minored in political science. During my last year, I worked in an aerosol research group at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography.

    After graduating in 2012, I worked for a couple years as a research technician at the University of Texas in El Paso studying the neuroscience of drug addiction in rats. There I developed an interest in molecular biology and went off to graduate school at Duke University, where I joined Stefano Di Talia's lab in the Department of Cell Biology. My dissertation focused on the timing of cell divisions in the fly embryo.

    In 2020, my wife and I moved to Copenhagen, Denmark. I finished my PhD in 2022 and subsequently took a postdoctoral position studying workplace exposures to airborne particles (including plastics) at the National Research Center for the Working Environment. In 2024, I started a second PhD in philosophy of science.
