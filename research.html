I am interested broadly in the biomedical sciences: their histories, social formations, and epistemological innovations. I work primarily within philosophy of science in practice, science and technology studies (STS), and history and philosophy of science (HPS). My PhD project uses qualitative methods (ethnography and interviews) to examine how emerging technologies shape biomedical knowledge, with a focus on single-cell and spatial biology.
